SQL Injection is a web security vulnerability where attackers manipulate database queries by inserting malicious SQL code into input fields.

Cross Site Scripting (XSS) allows attackers to inject malicious scripts into webpages that are executed in other users' browsers.

Phishing is a social engineering attack where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card details.

Malware is malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems.

Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts a victim's files and demands payment to restore access.

A firewall is a network security system that monitors and controls incoming and outgoing traffic based on security rules.

A Denial of Service (DoS) attack floods a system with excessive requests, causing it to become unavailable to legitimate users.

A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack uses multiple systems to overwhelm a target server with traffic.

Brute force attacks attempt to guess passwords by trying many combinations until the correct one is found.

A Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) attack occurs when an attacker secretly intercepts communication between two parties.

Zero Trust Security is a security model that requires verification for every user and device accessing a system.

Privilege escalation occurs when an attacker gains higher-level permissions than originally allowed.

Trojan malware disguises itself as legitimate software to trick users into installing it.

Spyware secretly collects information from a computer system without the user's knowledge.

Adware displays unwanted advertisements and may track user behavior.

Botnets are networks of infected computers controlled by attackers to perform large-scale attacks.

Social engineering attacks manipulate people into revealing confidential information.

Password cracking involves recovering passwords using various techniques such as dictionary attacks or brute force.

Encryption converts data into a coded format to protect it from unauthorized access.

Multi-factor authentication (MFA) requires multiple verification methods to access a system.

Network intrusion detection systems monitor network traffic for suspicious activity.

Endpoint security protects individual devices such as computers and smartphones from cyber threats.

Data breaches occur when sensitive data is accessed or exposed without authorization.

Security patches are updates that fix vulnerabilities in software systems.

Penetration testing involves simulating cyberattacks to identify security weaknesses.

Hackers are individuals who use technical skills to find and exploit vulnerabilities in computer systems, networks, or applications.

Not all hackers are malicious. Some hackers work to identify weaknesses in systems so they can be fixed before criminals exploit them.

Understanding different types of hackers helps organizations better defend their systems and train employees to recognize cyber threats.

White hat hackers are ethical hackers who work to improve cybersecurity by identifying vulnerabilities in systems.

White hat hackers often work as penetration testers, security consultants, or internal security teams.

The goal of white hat hackers is to strengthen security by discovering weaknesses before malicious attackers can exploit them.

Black hat hackers are malicious actors who exploit vulnerabilities for financial gain, disruption, or sabotage.

Black hat hackers use techniques such as malware distribution, ransomware attacks, credential theft, and data breaches.

Black hat hackers are responsible for many cybercrimes including identity theft, financial fraud, and corporate espionage.

Grey hat hackers operate between ethical and malicious hacking.

Grey hat hackers may test systems without permission but usually do not intend to cause damage.

Sometimes grey hat hackers disclose discovered vulnerabilities publicly in order to force organizations to fix security flaws.

Hacktivists are hackers who use cyberattacks to promote political or social causes.

Hacktivists often conduct activities such as website defacement, data leaks, and service disruptions.

Hacktivist groups attempt to draw public attention to political or ideological issues through cyber activities.

State-sponsored hackers are government-backed cyber operators who conduct cyber espionage or cyber warfare.

Nation-state hackers often target critical infrastructure, government systems, and competing nations.

State-sponsored hackers are usually highly skilled, well funded, and capable of conducting long-term stealth operations.

Cyberterrorists use cyber attacks to create fear, chaos, or disruption in critical infrastructure.

Cyberterrorism may target power grids, financial systems, transportation networks, or government systems.

The goal of cyberterrorists is often to destabilize societies or promote ideological agendas.

Insider threats occur when trusted individuals within an organization misuse their access to harm systems or steal data.

Insider threats can come from employees, contractors, or partners who already have authorized system access.

Motivations for insider threats may include revenge, financial gain, coercion, or whistleblowing.

Social engineering attacks manipulate human psychology to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information.

Phishing attacks are a common form of social engineering where attackers impersonate trusted entities through emails or messages.

Security awareness training helps employees recognize cyber threats such as phishing, impersonation, and malicious links.

Employees are often the first line of defense against cyber attacks because many attacks target human behavior rather than technical systems.

Cybersecurity awareness programs train employees to identify suspicious emails, unusual requests, and potential security threats.

Combining security awareness training with penetration testing helps organizations identify both technical and human vulnerabilities.

Well-trained employees can significantly reduce the risk of successful cyber attacks by recognizing suspicious activities early.

Organizations that invest in cybersecurity education improve their overall security posture and resilience against cyber threats.
